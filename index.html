ANTONIA V. SANTANGELO



ABOUT ME


Antonia Santangelo grew up in Rahway, New Jersey training in dance at Union County Dancer Center for seven years. She attended the Wardlaw-Hartridge School for her middle school and high school years where she was an active member of the performing arts community, holding position as historian in the choir and performing as many leading roles in both musicals (Lola in "Damn Yankees") and plays (Romaine Vole in "Witness for the Prosecution). One of Antonia's favorite experiences was when she had the opportunity of being on a fly system for her role in "Tarzan" as the leopard. She also received the Elizabeth O'Horne award upon graduation for excellence in the dramatic arts. In her spare time you could find Antonia on the competition mat for cheerleading. Her junior year the Wardlaw-Hartrdige cheerleaders were state champions and Antonia became co-captain her senior year. Antonia also spent three years with the Pineda Conservatory where she had workshops with people like Beth Malone and Michelle McConnell. She played roles in "On the Town" (Madame Dilly/dance ensemble) and "The Wedding Singer" (Linda). Antonia is currently a student at the American Musical and Dramatic Academy in New York, or, AMDA. She is receiving a certificate for musical theatre February 2018 and will be continuing to the LA program to receive her BFA in performing arts (graduation 2020). She has had the pleasure of working with Chryssie Whitehead, Dan Dailey, Casey Colgan and many more. Antonia would like to thank her teachers for teaching her to accept who she is and what she can bring to the table. There is nobody who does "you" better than you!
